Jargon Buster

We know that finding a new card for your business can be difficult.
There is nothing more important to American Express than our customers
finding the best card to suit their needs, so we have created our
'Jargon Buster' to help Cardmembers understand AMEX's financial terminology.

Annual Percentage Rate - a percentage which shows the yearly cost
to borrow money. This percentage includes the interest expense, and
all other fees associated with obtaining the loan.

Represeted as a percentage, the interest rate is the amount charged
to the borrower for taking out a loan.

Unlike a charge card, the user can pay off their purchases over time,
however interest will be charged.

Displayed as an interest rate, interest is what the lender charges the
the borrower for borrowing money, or interest is charged if repayment is
delayed.

The maximum amount of money that the user can spend in a specified
time frame. If the user reaches their spending limit, the card will
decline regardless if there are enough funds in the user's account.
Such spending limits differ, and are set by the institution which the
